About Lego Belville

LEGO Belville was introduced in 1994 with the goal of inspiring imagination and creativity play in children. Originally founded by Ole Kirk Kristiansen, LEGO's goal has always been to fuel creativity and encourage endless free play. LEGO Belville was specifically designed as a toy line aimed at girls, featuring our famous multi-jointed dollies and a signature color scheme of soft pinks and purples.

LEGO Belville released 88 sets in total, including charming creations such as playhouses, princess rooms, play-parks, nurseries, and many more! These sets were designed to allow children to build and explore magical, imaginative worlds. While only a few sets are displayed here, you can find many more at your local retailer, each offering a unique way to create stories and adventures!

Though LEGO Belville was discontinued in 2009, its spirit of imaginative play continued through our successor, LEGO Friends, which was introduced in 2012. LEGO Friends picked up where Belville left off, offering girls a new way to engage in creative building and play with modern characters and settings.
